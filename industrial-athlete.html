Industrial Athlete

We focus not only on the safety of the Industrial Athlete, but also their health and happiness.

We enable Employers to make their Employees not only feel that they are a producer of goods or services, but also are treating them like the Industrial Athlete they truly are.

Like any “Athlete” they perform a job that requires a wide range of physical demands, such as strength, endurance, flexibility, and coordination.

1. Heat Stress

Preventing heat stress and promoting hydration and rest breaks in high-temperature work environments can reduce the risk of heat-related illnesses and improve worker productivity. By implementing these measures, companies can create a safer and more comfortable work environment and enhance employee well-being.

2. Fatigue Recommendations

Assessing and managing the risk of fatigue is essential for promoting productivity and safety in the workplace. Incorporating carbohydrates into meals and snacks can be an effective short-term fatigue-reducing activity for workers. By providing workers with carbohydrate-rich food options and educating them on the benefits of incorporating carbs into their diet, employers can help reduce the risk of fatigue, improve energy levels, and enhance overall workplace performance.

3. Musculoskeletal Recommendations

Incorporating daily stretch and flex exercises tailored to workers' individual needs can help reduce the risk of musculoskeletal injuries in the workplace. Our tailored programs are designed to promote injury prevention and safety, with exercises specific to each microsegment by location. By regularly engaging in these exercises, workers can reduce stress, increase energy levels, and improve their cardiovascular health, even if their job involves extended periods of sitting, standing, or climbing.
